A Guide to Seated Dip Machines

The seated dip machine is a versatile piece of equipment designed to target the triceps, chest, and shoulders. It provides a controlled environment to perform dips, reducing the risk of injury and ensuring proper form.

What is a Seated Dip Machine?

Here are some safety tips for using a seated dip machine:

  • Adjust the seat height to ensure a comfortable range of motion.
  • Select a weight that allows for controlled movements.
  • Keep your back against the backrest throughout the exercise.
  • Avoid locking your elbows at the bottom of the movement.

Here's How to Use a Dip Machine

Standard Seated Dip

The standard seated dip is effective for targeting the triceps. To perform the standard seated dip:

  • Sit with your back against the backrest and feet flat on the floor.
  • Grip the handles and press down until your arms are fully extended.
  • Slowly return to the starting position.
  • Repeat 12 to 15 times for one set.
